You know those AI helpers that write code for you (like Claude Code)? They're great — but sometimes lazy. Treats gives you a little animal on your screen. When the AI does a good job, you pet it with your mouse 🖐️; when it slips, you give it a stern look. Somehow the AI notices, and tries harder next time.

Pet one yourself ↓ Get it on GitHub

Wait — what's a "treat"? 🦴

A treat is just a point. Like a gold star a teacher puts in a notebook, or a snack you give a dog for sitting.

Where does it come from?You give it. You're the trainer; Claude is the puppy. There's no money and nothing to buy — you're just keeping score.
What happens on a "bad dog"?One point comes off the score. Nothing is sent anywhere; the number simply goes down. Low enough and your AI lands in the "doghouse."
Why bother?Because your score is quietly shown back to Claude every time you ask it something. So it sees "you've been scolded for skipping tests" — and stops skipping tests.
Do I need to be a programmer?To use Claude Code, a little. To use Treats on top of it, no — you mostly just click a cute animal with your mouse.

How it works, in 3 steps

1

Claude does some work

You ask Claude Code to build or fix something, like you normally would. It writes the code.

2

You pet it — or scold it

Happy with the work? Pet the animal with your mouse 🖐️. Not happy? Give it a stern right-click. That's it — there's nothing to type. (A keyboard shortcut works too, if you like.)

3

The AI tries harder

Before the AI answers you next time, it quietly gets a little note about how it's been doing — so it cleans up its act. That's the whole trick.

It runs quietly in the background. You set it up once, then forget it's there — until you reach over and pet the little guy.

What you get

        

          
🖐️ Pet it with your mouse
A little animal lives in your screen corner. Click to treat, right-click to scold, drag it anywhere. No commands to memorize.

          
🔊 It's alive
It barks (or meows, or rawrs) when you treat it, whimpers when scolded, wiggles on its own, and curls up to sleep 💤 when you're idle.

          
🧠 It actually sticks
Your reactions are passed back to the AI, so a treat or a scolding changes how it works — not just how you feel.

          
✅ Rewards good work by itself
When the AI runs your tests and they pass, the pet earns a treat on its own. So it learns that keeping things working pays off — no clicking needed.

          
🎓 Teaches good habits
It gently steers the AI to plan before coding, write tests, and double-check its own work — all framed as ways to earn treats.

          
⌨️ Or just say the word
Don't want to reach for the mouse? A quick /treats:good or /treats:bad works too. Totally optional.

          
⌨️ One-key shortcut
Hands on the keyboard? ⌘⇧G (or Ctrl+Shift+G) gives a treat, ⌘⇧B scolds — from anywhere, with a happy chime or a thud.

          
🐾 Always in view
Your animal can also stroll along the bottom of the screen while you work, showing its score — a tiny pet that's always there: 🦴 ··🐶·· +3.

          
🏆 Ranks & report cards
It climbs from Good Pup to Best Boy when you're pleased, or slides down to Bad Dog and the Doghouse when you're not. With weekly report cards.

          
📁 One pet, many projects
Working on several things at once? Each project gets its own score, and the pet shows whichever one you're currently working on.

          
🔒 Private & yours
It all lives in a little file on your own computer. No accounts, no sign-up, no money, nothing sent anywhere.

          
↩︎ Forgiving
Pressed the wrong button? treats undo takes it right back.

Get started

        
You'll need Claude Code — the AI coding helper Treats sits on top of. Then pick a path. Don't worry if the lines below look like gibberish: copy, paste, press enter.


        
🖐️ The full pet (macOS)

        
Paste these one at a time — then a little animal pops up in the corner:

        
git clone https://github.com/0xcnr0/treats
cd treats
npm install
node packages/core/bin/treats.js install
npm run pet

        
Click the pet → treat · right-click → scold · drag it anywhere. That's the whole thing.


        
⚡ The 10-second version (any computer)

        
No pet, but the AI still learns from you. Inside Claude Code, type these two lines:

        
/plugin marketplace add 0xcnr0/treats
/plugin install treats

        
Then just say /treats:good or /treats:bad whenever you like. Everything sets itself up.

      


      
      

        
Common questions

        

          
Is anything sent to the cloud?No. Your score lives in a single plain file on your own computer (~/.treats). There are no accounts, no sign-up, and nothing is uploaded anywhere. The only thing Claude ever sees is the short note about how it's been doing — and that's already part of your own Claude session.

          
Does it slow Claude down?No noticeable difference. The note Claude reads is just a few lines of text, and the score is read from a tiny local file. There's no network call and no heavy work added to your requests.

          
How do I uninstall it?The pet is just an app you can quit and delete. To remove the Claude hooks, restore the timestamped backup that install-hooks printed: cp ~/.claude/settings.json.backup.<timestamp> ~/.claude/settings.json. Your ~/.treats file can be deleted any time.

          
Does it cost anything?No. Treats is free and open source (MIT). There's no money in it — a "treat" is just a point you keep for yourself. It's not affiliated with Anthropic.
